Frequently Asked Questions

We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (validated by NIH) combined with your activity multiplier to calculate your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). For fat loss, we apply a 300–500 kcal deficit; for muscle gain, a 300–500 kcal surplus. All calorie targets shown in your plan are personalised to your inputs.

Macronutrients — Protein, Carbohydrates, and Fat — are the three energy-providing nutrients in food. Tracking them matters because the ratio directly affects body composition: protein preserves and builds muscle, carbs fuel exercise and the brain, and fats support hormone production. Our plans follow NASM-recommended ratios tailored to your body type.

Scroll up to the Log Nutrition section. Type any food name in the search bar — our database auto-suggests matching items. Enter the quantity in grams, then click Add Food. The entry appears in your daily log with its calorie and macro breakdown instantly calculated.

NASM recognises three body types: Ectomorph (lean, fast metabolism — needs more calories & carbs), Mesomorph (naturally athletic — balanced macros & mixed training), and Endomorph (higher fat storage tendency — needs a deficit, lower carbs, HIIT). Your plan's meals, macros, and workout tips are all customised based on your type.

General guidelines recommend 2.5–3.5 litres per day for most adults, and more on training days. Our Water Tracker (top right card) helps you hit your goal — add intake using the +250 ml / +500 ml buttons. Your plan page also shows a specific water target personalised to your body type, as recommended by NASM nutrition guidelines.
